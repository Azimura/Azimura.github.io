CyberPatriot

CyberPatriot is a yearly competition that is created by the AFA. CyberPatriot is a competition where students will form teams and compete against other teams throughout the nation to see who could make a computer/network more "secure". I joined our school's CyberPatriot program when I was in 8th grade. I formed a team with 4 other people, and in that team I was the Windows specialist. Being the Windows specialist, I had to learn a lot about the many settings in most of the Windows operating system. These include, Windows 10, Windows 8/8.1, Windows 7, Windows Server 2012, and Windows Server 2016. In addition to learning about the operating system, I had to learn about the security settings of many programs such as Firefox, Internet Explorer 11, and FileZilla. I also learned about batch scripting, which is a coding language used to change Windows Setting via command prompt. During my 8th grade year, My team was able to place 1st out of 8 middle school teams in Ohio and 21st out of 2,000 middle school teams in the nation. In my 9th grade year, My team was able to place 2nd place highschool team in Ohio in the gold tier, and during the semifinals we were beat the other highschool team in Ohio and placed 8th out of nation in the gold tier. During my 10th grade year, I had joined a new team and we were slotted into the platinum tier where we had placed first in state during the state round and 32nd nationally out of 221 Platinum teams. During my 11th grade year, we had a similar preformance to that of our 10th grade year. We were placed into the platinum tier and won first in state during the 3rd round, and then we placed 39th in the nation out of 917 platinum tier teams.

Terracycle

I was also in our schools Green Team. While I was in Green Team, I helped with the Terracycle group. In Terracycle, we would collect the trash bags from the Terracycle bin and brought them back into Mr.Nelson's (now Ms.Carter's) classroom. Then we would sort out the terracycle into 6 different categories. These categories include juice pouches, chip bags, garnola bars, gogo squeezes, little bites, and peronal hygine. To figure out if something could be terracycled, we would check in the inside to see if it had foil lining. If it did, chances were that it could be terracycled. After sorting everything, we would shipped out the things that could be terracycled.
